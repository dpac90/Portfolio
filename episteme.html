Episteme

Connect. Collaborate. Contribute.

Date: May 2014 - April 2015
Client: Fortune 500 IT Consulting Company
Role: Lead UX Designer
Deliverable: Resposive Web Portal

Problem Summary

There was an increasing need for better collaboration among one of the company's largest departments with 30,000+ employees and counting. Business Analysts discovered that even though Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) were at large, it was getting increasingly difficult to identify and reach them for advice on client-specific requirements and to obtain training. Since most of the information would be client-sensitive and confidential, the company wanted to build an intranet site that would help employees collaborate, and was unwilling to use public corporate social networks.

A web-based intranet forum geared to solve this provlem already existed. However, the forum's adoption rate was low due to poor User Experience (UX).

Goal

The primary goal was to revamp and improve the UX of the existing web-based forum, while adding features that would attract users, increase interaction among the users, and retain them.

The business goal on the other hand, was to ultimately enable employees to cater to the needs of their clients in the best way possible. Episteme was poised to serve this goal through increased collaboration among employees that interact with clients and information sharing.

Approach

Episteme was envisioned as a platform that would enable employees to connect, collaborate and contribute. This project focuses on the web-based portal, which is only a part of that vision, that would have the following features:

  • Forum - A place for people to ask questions directly to SMEs
  • Store - A platform to showcase in-house tools and products
  • Tech Talk - A repository and showcase of conferences and knowledge sharing sessions conducted by employees
  • Periodiko - A magazine-format blog for thought-leaders to share their ideas
  • Engage - A platform to manage interactions with real clients

To build a better system that served its users well, I had to understand the technology being used; front-end as well as back-end. The existing web forum was created using C# and the .NET framework, and had a very dated UI that utilized HTML tables for the layout. The technology used also did not allow flexibility for content changes and quick UI improvements. To allow for better content management, a complete overhaul of the system architecture was needed, while keeping in mind that all the existing data would have to be ported to the new system. The UI also needed to be modern and responsive, so users can connect and collaborate using multiple devices.

However, we overcame this challenge by conducting regular conference calls, keeping connected through IM and social messaging services, and occasionally visiting each others' offices.

Conclusion

The result of this project was a modern, responsive and user-friendly web portal that allowed users to collaborate and stay updated with latest developments in the department. However, one glaring exclusion from the project process was user research and user evaluations. We faced a lot of issues due to lack of knowledge about what our users really wanted. We also did high fidelity prototypes, which meant it was harder to incorporate feedback from the stakeholders.

After this project I learnt the value of doing user research early on, and to prefer low fidelity prototyping over high fidelity ones, especially in the beginning stages.

Business goals were met as follows:

  • User base increased by 600% within 3 months of launch
  • Average weekly user activity in Forum went up by 500% within 2 months of launch
  • Improved exposure and client engagement opportunities for in-house products through Store
  • Improved knowledge sharing via employee blog posts in Periodiko and conference on Tech Talk
  • Improved client interaction management with centralized repository of client engagement records via Engage

Note: Content and images were modified to remove client-sensitive and confidential information.
