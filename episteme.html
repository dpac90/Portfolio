Episteme

Connect. Collaborate. Contribute.

Period: May 2014 - April 2015
Client: Fortune 500 IT Consulting Company
Role: Lead UI Designer / Front-end Developer
Project Type: Responsive Web Portal

Introduction

Episteme is an internal web platform that consists of the following modules:
  • Forum
    Post questions and get answers from Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)
  • Store
    Showcase in-house tools and products
  • Tech Talk
    Knowledge sharing sessions and conferences
  • Periodiko
    Share ideas and perspectives
  • Engage
    Manage and track interactions with client projects

Problem Summary

  • There was an increasing need for better collaboration among one of the company's largest departments with 30,000+ employees

Tools and Technologies used:

Results

  • User base increased by 600% within 3 months of launch
  • Average weekly user activity in Forum went up by 500% within 2 months of launch
  • Improved exposure and client engagement opportunities for in-house products through Store
  • Improved knowledge sharing via employee blog posts in Periodiko and conferences on Tech Talk
  • Improved client interaction management with centralized repository of client engagement records via Engage

Conclusion

  • The result was a modern, responsive and user-friendly web portal that allowed users to collaborate and stay up-to-date with latest developments in the department.
  • One major exclusion from the project process, however, was user research and user evaluations. We made a lot of assumptions about what our users wanted, which meant we had to make plenty of revisions in the development stage. We also did high fidelity prototypes, which meant it was harder to incorporate feedback from the stakeholders.
  • After this project I learned the value of doing user research early on, and to prefer low fidelity prototyping over high fidelity ones, especially in the early stages.
