Walgreens

Pharmacy Chat

Period: November 2017 - March 2018
Company: Walgreens
Role: User Experience Designer
Project Type: Native iOS / Android Apps

Introduction

  • Walgreens Pharmacy Chat provides customers seeking medical prescription assistance 24/7 access to chat with pharmacists
  • It is commonly used by customers to address:
    • Prescription status and refills
    • Medication questions
    • Health advice
    • Pricing and insurance

Problem Summary

  • Existing implementation was web-based, and the experience was not optimized for mobile. This was a critical issue, as more than 90% of customers were accessing chat from a mobile device (native app + mobile web)
  • When customers needed to reference their medications or status, they could not minimize the chat but were forced to close the webview, end the current session, and start a new one

Results

  • Helped Walgreens take a step towards their mission of giving more significance to the “Voice of the Pharmacist” by building a sleek chat interface to access pharmacy professionals 24/7
  • Contextual placements greatly improved the utility of, and interaction with, Pharmacy Chat. We saw that the drug information page was one of the top places in which Pharmacy Chat was accessed

Retrospective

  • The initial design workshop I facilitated could’ve been more focused. We gathered several broad ideas using How Might We’s and Crazy 8s, but it was difficult and time-consuming to narrow them down later due to lack of initial focus
  • Earlier collaboration with visual designers would’ve helped to ensure my wireframes were in line with any visual patterns we already use in the app
  • I introduced the team to Zeplin, a collaboration tool for design handoffs. Although we were not able to adopt it as far as developer usage, it greatly helped my collaboration with visual designers to create the hi-fi prototype
